our approach

Our clients' needs guide us. We realize that each client has different needs and we will ensure you have the proper resources before, during and after the completion of your legal work.

after hours appointments

Legal concerns and legal processes can be intimidating and we know that. You may have old questions that have been in the back of your mind for some time or you may have new questions because something has changed in your life recently such as a marriage, divorce, or the death of a loved one. These are challenging situations and we are here to help.


We have after-hours available every Tuesday from 5-6 PM so feel free to call and schedule an in- person meeting with an attorney for a free consultation. Appointments will be taken on a first come first serve basis so if you have a short window of time please call ahead.

we are just a phone call away

Questions come up and circumstances change - life happens. We are available via telephone, video conference or for in-person visit (with Covid precautions in place) to discuss your situation.

our attorneys

Kimberly J. Long Hooper, Esq.

Kim grew up in Westford, graduating from WA. She spent her summers in Rhode Island and earned her B.A. in Psychology from URI. After working in the environmental field and traveling, she returned to Massachusetts to work as a Social Worker at DSS, completing the Domestic Violence Training Program at the Alternative House in Lowell.


Kim has worked for the last 20 years in the field of law, graduating Magna Cum Laude from the Massachusetts School of Law. In addition, she has served on local church boards, school committees and community organizations in Haverhill and Amesbury where she and her husband raised their two boys as well as became foster parents.


Kim remains passionate about helping people, providing quality professional service, and compassionate care. With a background in estate planning, real estate law, probate, landlord tenant law, and personal injury, Kim looks forward to meeting new clients to best assist them with their estate planning and probate administration needs.

Julie Johnson, Esq.

Julie earned her Bachelor of Science degree in Management from Boston University and is a Cum Laude graduate of Massachusetts School of Law. Julie’s practice focuses in the areas of estate planning, trust and estate administration and settlement, probate, real estate (seller representation), estate tax return preparation and uncontested guardianships and conservatorships.


Julie is a member of the National Academy of Elder Law Attorneys—including its Massachusetts Chapter—as well as the Massachusetts Bar Association. Julie speaks at local Senior Centers (pre-Covid) on the essentials of estate planning.


Julie currently serves on the Board of Directors for Aging Services of North Central Massachusetts (formerly known as Montachusett Home Care Corporation) and, in the past, served on both the Zoning Board of Appeals and the Planning Board in Townsend, where she has resided for the past 21 years with her husband, Eric, and their two daughters.
